Nutrition Science and Healthy Eating

Nutrition is the science of how food affects the health and functioning of the human body. Proper nutrition provides the body with essential nutrients including carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, and water necessary for growth and maintenance.

Macronutrients are nutrients required in large amounts for energy and bodily functions. Carbohydrates are the primary energy source for the brain and muscles. Proteins are essential for tissue repair, immune function, and enzyme production. Dietary fats support cell membrane structure and hormone production.

Micronutrients include vitamins and minerals needed in smaller quantities but critical for health. Vitamin C supports immune function and collagen synthesis. Iron is essential for oxygen transport in the blood. Calcium and vitamin D maintain strong bones and teeth throughout life.

The Mediterranean diet emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, olive oil, and moderate fish consumption. Research shows this dietary pattern reduces the risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and certain cancers. Plant-based diets are increasingly popular for health and environmental reasons.

Obesity is a growing global health concern linked to excessive caloric intake and sedentary lifestyles. It increases the risk of heart disease, diabetes, hypertension, and certain cancers. Balanced nutrition combined with regular physical activity is essential for maintaining a healthy body weight.
