Climate Change and Global Warming

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns across the Earth. While climate change can occur naturally, human activities have been the main driver since the 1800s, primarily due to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas.

Global warming is the long-term heating of Earth's surface observed since the pre-industrial period. It is primarily caused by human activities, especially fossil fuel burning, which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth's atmosphere.

The greenhouse effect occurs when the Sun's energy reaches the Earth's atmosphere. Some of this energy is reflected back to space, but some is absorbed and re-radiated by greenhouse gases. Carbon dioxide, methane, and water vapor are the primary greenhouse gases.

Climate change impacts include rising sea levels, more frequent extreme weather events, melting polar ice caps, and shifts in wildlife habitats. Many species face extinction due to rapid environmental changes caused by global warming.

Renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power are critical alternatives to fossil fuels. Transitioning to clean energy is essential to reduce carbon emissions and mitigate the worst effects of climate change on our planet.
