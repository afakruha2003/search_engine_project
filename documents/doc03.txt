Space Exploration and Astronomy

Space exploration is the investigation of outer space using astronomy and space technology. The early era of space exploration was driven largely by a space race between the Soviet Union and the United States during the Cold War.

The first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, was launched by the Soviet Union in 1957. NASA was established in 1958 and has led numerous landmark missions, including the Apollo moon landings. Neil Armstrong became the first human to walk on the Moon in 1969.

Mars exploration has become a major focus of space agencies worldwide. NASA's rovers, including Curiosity and Perseverance, have collected valuable data about Martian geology, atmosphere, and the potential for ancient microbial life on the red planet.

The James Webb Space Telescope represents a major leap in astronomy, allowing scientists to observe galaxies billions of light years away. It can detect infrared light from the first galaxies formed after the Big Bang, providing unprecedented views of the early universe.

Private companies like SpaceX and Blue Origin are transforming space exploration by developing reusable rockets and planning missions to Mars. Commercial spaceflight is opening opportunities for space tourism and satellite deployment.
