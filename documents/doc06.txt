Blockchain Technology and Cryptocurrency

Blockchain is a distributed ledger technology that records transactions across multiple computers in a way that is transparent, secure, and immutable. Each block in the chain contains transaction data, a timestamp, and a cryptographic hash of the previous block.

Bitcoin was the first cryptocurrency, created in 2009 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto. It operates on a peer-to-peer network without central authority. Bitcoin uses proof-of-work consensus mechanism where miners compete to validate transactions and earn rewards.

Ethereum is a blockchain platform that enables smart contracts and decentralized applications. Smart contracts are self-executing contracts with terms directly written into code. They automatically enforce and execute agreements without requiring intermediaries.

Decentralized finance, known as DeFi, uses blockchain technology to recreate traditional financial services like lending, borrowing, and trading without central institutions. Non-fungible tokens, or NFTs, represent ownership of unique digital assets using blockchain verification.

Cryptocurrency markets are highly volatile and speculative. Regulatory frameworks for digital currencies are evolving worldwide as governments seek to balance innovation with consumer protection and financial stability requirements.
