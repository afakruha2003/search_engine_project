Robotics and Industrial Automation

Robotics is an interdisciplinary field combining computer science, engineering, and technology to design, build, and operate robots. Robots are programmable machines capable of performing tasks automatically or semi-automatically, often replacing or assisting human workers.

Industrial robots have transformed manufacturing by performing repetitive tasks with high precision and speed. Automotive assembly lines use robotic arms for welding, painting, and part assembly. These machines improve productivity while reducing human error and workplace injuries.

Autonomous robots can perform tasks without human intervention by perceiving their environment and making decisions. Self-driving vehicles use sensors, cameras, and machine learning algorithms to navigate roads safely. Drone technology enables autonomous aerial delivery and surveillance operations.

Collaborative robots, or cobots, are designed to work alongside humans in shared spaces. Unlike traditional industrial robots that operate in caged environments, cobots use sensors to detect human presence and adjust their movements accordingly for safe interaction.

Artificial intelligence is increasingly integrated into robotics, enabling machines to learn from experience and adapt to new situations. Service robots are entering homes, hospitals, and restaurants to assist with tasks like cleaning, patient care, and food delivery worldwide.
